## 🧠 Step 1: Pick the Right “Main Subject” for Alpha

Since you want the app to feel *substantial*, pick a subject that’s:

✅ High demand among UPSC aspirants
✅ Conceptually rich but not too vast for content creation
✅ Easy to expand later into both Prelims + Mains overlap

### Best choice: **Indian Polity**

**Why:**

* It’s foundational (appears in both Prelims & Mains GS Paper II).
* Students love revising it frequently.
* Easy to modularize into chapters/tests.
* Perfect for building both objective (Prelims-style) and subjective (Mains-style) questions.

## 🧭 UPSC Exam Structure Overview

UPSC has **3 stages** — Prelims, Mains, and Interview.

Your test series app would mainly cover **Prelims and Mains** (Interview is subjective and can be later added as mentorship).

---

### **1️⃣ Preliminary Examination (Objective Type)**

Two papers — both conducted on same day.

| Paper                               | Subject                                                                          | Marks | Duration | Notes            |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----- | -------- | ---------------- |
| **Paper I – General Studies (GS)**  | Current Affairs, History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Environment, Science, etc. | 200   | 2 hours  | Counts for merit |
| **Paper II – CSAT (Aptitude Test)** | Comprehension, Reasoning, Maths, Decision Making                                 | 200   | 2 hours  | Qualifying (33%) |

#### ✅ You can make **2 Prelims test series types:**

* Full-length GS Paper I mock tests (based on yearly pattern)
* Full-length CSAT tests + sectional quizzes

You can also add **Topic-wise mini tests**:

* History (Ancient/Medieval/Modern)
* Polity
* Geography
* Environment
* Economy
* Current Affairs (Monthly/Weekly)

So roughly **8–10 subjects for Prelims**, each with sectional + full tests.

---

### **2️⃣ Mains Examination (Descriptive Type)**

Total **9 papers** (7 count for merit, 2 qualifying).

| Paper     | Subject                                                                 | Marks | Notes            |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----- | ---------------- |
| Paper A   | Indian Language                                                         | 300   | Qualifying       |
| Paper B   | English                                                                 | 300   | Qualifying       |
| Paper I   | Essay                                                                   | 250   | Counts for merit |
| Paper II  | GS Paper I – Indian Heritage & Culture, History, Geography              | 250   |                  |
| Paper III | GS Paper II – Polity, Governance, IR                                    | 250   |                  |
| Paper IV  | GS Paper III – Economy, Science, Tech, Environment, Disaster Management | 250   |                  |
| Paper V   | GS Paper IV – Ethics, Integrity, Aptitude                               | 250   |                  |
| Paper VI  | Optional Paper I                                                        | 250   |                  |
| Paper VII | Optional Paper II                                                       | 250   |                  |

---

#### ✅ You can make **Mains test series types:**

**A. General Studies (4 Papers → 4 Test Series)**
Each paper can have 5–10 mock tests following UPSC pattern.

**B. Essay Test Series (1)**
Weekly essay writing challenge with AI/mentor evaluation.

---

### **3️⃣ Total Subject Areas to Cover in App**

| Category             | Approx. Subject/Test Areas            |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Prelims – GS Paper I | 8–10 (History, Polity, Economy, etc.) |
| Prelims – CSAT       | 3 (Quant, Reasoning, Comprehension)   |
| Mains – GS I–IV      | 4                                     |
| Mains – Essay        | 1                                     |

➡️ **So total: around 15–20 core subject categories** to begin with.
You can later expand to **35–40 test series types** including optionals, topic-wise, and full-length mocks.
